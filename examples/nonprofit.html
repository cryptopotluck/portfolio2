Supported Transportation

Bikes Save Lives
With real noticeable advances in technology the tools are finally available to offer independent transportation at a cost-effective rate. Our goal is to partner with people struggling in the community that do not have a reliable way to get around town and get them an electric bike and a path forward.

How does the nonprofit work?

  • Through our partnerships with the city, chamber of commerce, schools & community facilities. We work directly with the community to find people struggling and offer those individuals temporary supported transportation through our electric bikes & other support services.

  • Each bike we build is locally made and maintained and is set up with tracking to indicate important factors like current location and amount of time its being used.

  • We work with individuals directly so that we can provide an important stepping stone for greater independence. We supply a reliable way to get to a job, grab food, do laundry or offer an escape from a bad home situation.

  • As time progresses we aim to create a strong enough foundation for an individuals where they don't need the bike anymore so that we can get the bike in new hands and offer another person a path forward through our supported transportation network and scale the nonprofit forward.

This nonprofit originates from Damian Dragon a friend who lost his life to hardship & mental health problems. When working a summer job at a resort I met Damian, and we became friends early on. He was only two years younger than me but seemed so much farther behind, between mowing and maintained the grounds I noticed his work ethic and kind spirit. Early on in his life, he was shaped from a brutal past thrown to the streets and traveled between foster homes. His struggles persisted to early adulthood, and he was never able to find independence as he lived his remaining days working terrible jobs for minimum wage and biking around town from copono. Retrospectively, the right investment and structured support into Damian's life could have saved him from his suicide, and it's one of the reasons why I take this initiative today to help those struggling within the community.





We've partnered with established leaders in the community to expand and maintain our support services. We are thankful for the city of Rockport, the schools, Chamber of Comers & Coastal Plains Community Center for the resources and support you provide.

We would like to continue to grow our partnerships to individual small businesses in the area & to other counties that could benefit from our supported transportation network.
